My Projects

Olokunbobade Management System (Java + SQL)

The Olokunbobade Management System is an application designed to streamline the organization and management of data for my family’s annual reunion. This project reflects my commitment to solving real-world challenges while honoring the connections that matter most. The system offers a range of features tailored to enhance efficiency and accessibility. Users can add new family members, retrieve birthdays, display family groups under the Olokunbobade umbrella, manage T-shirt orders and sizes, confirm attendance, and track reunion dues, including payment statuses. In addition, the system is designed to provide a more personal touch by integrating with WhatsApp. For example, when viewing a family member's birthday, the system can prompt the user with the option to send a birthday message. If the user chooses to proceed, it will seamlessly redirect them to WhatsApp, ready to send a personalized message. This feature not only fosters stronger connections within the family but also adds convenience by leveraging widely used communication tools. These features address common pain points in event coordination and ensures a smooth experience for all users. Recognizing the diverse technical abilities within my family, I prioritized creating a user-friendly interface that accommodates everyone, including older relatives who may have less experience with technology. The design emphasizes simplicity and intuitive navigation, making the system accessible to all users. This project was developed using SQL and Java, ensuring data management and real-time updates. Any new information entered by users is immediately reflected in the database, promong accuracy, and reducing manual effort. This application not only demonstrates my technical skills but also highlights my ability to create meaningful, impacful solutions tailored to specific needs. The Management System reflects my dedication to using technology to bring people together and strengthen connections.

Brunswick Emergency Beacon (UX/UI + Emergency Preparedness App)

The Brunswick Emergency Beacon (BEB) is a community-centered application developed to improve emergency preparedness and communication in Brunswick, Missouri—a town historically impacted by natural disasters like river flooding, fires, and tornadoes. This project was born out of a real-world need for accessible and timely emergency alerts in rural areas where communication infrastructure is often limited. The app is designed to provide residents with critical safety updates and resources during crises. It features clear tab-based navigation for different disaster types, including floods, fires, and tornadoes. Each section offers preparation graphics, evacuation routes, designated shelter locations, and step-by-step safety guidelines. In addition, the app includes a contact list for local emergency services, making vital support easily reachable when seconds count. This project reflects our team’s commitment to human-centered design and collaborative problem-solving. We worked closely with town leadership, emergency responders, and community stakeholders to identify the most urgent needs and tailor the app accordingly. Our user experience emphasized clarity, simplicity, and fast access to life-saving information. The Brunswick Emergency Beacon not only showcases my contributions as a UX/UI designer but also highlights my ability to apply technology for social good. From research to prototyping and design, this project demonstrates how thoughtful design can empower communities and foster resilience in the face of natural disasters.
